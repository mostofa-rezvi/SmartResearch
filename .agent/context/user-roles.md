# User Roles & Permissions: The Four Kinds of People

ResearchBridge implements a hierarchical permission structure that reflects real-world academic hierarchy and responsibility.

## 1. Super Admin
- **Role**: Platform operator / Administrator.
- **Capabilities**: Can see everything and configure everything.
- **Exclusive Powers**: Sole ability to formally invite special external members (Invited Users). Handles disputes, manages platform-wide settings, and has full visibility into all activity.

## 2. Admin
- **Role**: Operational backbone.
- **Capabilities**: Handles day-to-day moderation and management.
- **Specific Tasks**: Approves flagged content, manages journal listings, oversees community groups, and supports users facing issues.

## 3. Users (Standard)
The heart of ResearchBridge. Divides into context-based distinctions (not strict hierarchy) to serve relevant recommendations:
- **New Researcher**: Has not yet published work or is beginning their academic journey (e.g., student, curious amateur).
- **Amateur Researcher**: Actively working (e.g., graduate student mid-thesis, independent researcher, contributor to collaborative studies).
- **Profile Identity**: Acts as a living academic CV, surfacing interests, contributions (Q&A/Posts), saved papers, and a platform activity timeline.

## 4. Invited Users
- **Role**: Verified external experts (professors, research supervisors, senior academics).
- **Onboarding**: Do not sign up on their own; solely rely on a formal invitation process initiated by the Super Admin.
- **Profile Identity**: Credible representation of academic standing, including professional affiliations, academic rank, supervised students, and editorial roles. Stands alongside institutional faculty pages.

## Visibility & Context
Profiles are globally visible. Clicking a user's name in the community allows immediate understanding of their career stage and expertise, ensuring interactions are respectful and appropriately weighted.
