## Phase 1: Journals (Days 1–2) — Easiest Win

This is 100% free and publicly available. You don't need to manually collect 1000 journals — **download them in bulk**.

### Free Authoritative Sources

**Scimago Journal Rankings (SJR)** — Best source
- Go to: `scimagojr.com/journalrank.php`
- Filter by: Subject area, Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4, Year
- **Export as CSV** → You get thousands of journals instantly
- Fields included: Journal name, ISSN, Publisher, H-index, Quartile, Subject area, Country

**Clarivate/Web of Science JCR**
- `jcr.clarivate.com` (needs institutional access OR use your university login)
- Gives Impact Factor + Quartile data

**DOAJ (Directory of Open Access Journals)**
- `doaj.org/csv` → Free bulk download, 20,000+ journals
- Great for open-access researchers

**Scopus Source List**
- Google: *"Scopus source list 2024 download"*
- Elsevier publishes this as an Excel file publicly every year

### Day 1–2 Action
```
1. Download Scimago CSV → filter top 1000 by citations
2. Download DOAJ CSV → merge unique journals
3. Tag each with: Field, Quartile, ISSN, Publisher, Submission URL
4. Load into your DB — this is bulk insert, not manual entry
```

## Phase 2: Researchers & Professors (Days 2–4) — Requires Strategy

You **cannot scrape** LinkedIn or Academia.edu. Use these legitimate approaches:

### Source 1: OpenAlex (Best Free API)
- `openalex.org` — Open scholarly graph with 250M+ works
- **API endpoint:** `api.openalex.org/authors?filter=cited_by_count:>1000`
- Returns: Name, Institution, Research topics, Citation count, ORCID, recent publications
- **Free, no auth needed for basic use**
- You can pull 100+ researchers per domain in minutes

```javascript
// Example API call
fetch("https://api.openalex.org/authors?filter=last_known_institution.country_code:US,cited_by_count:>500&per-page=50&page=1")
```

### Source 2: ORCID Public Data
- `pub.orcid.org` — Public researcher profiles
- Download the **ORCID Public Data File** (torrent, ~30GB) OR use their API
- API: `pub.orcid.org/v3.0/search/?q=keyword:machine+learning&rows=100`
- Returns: ORCID iD, name, affiliation, works

### Source 3: Google Scholar Profiles (Manual but Targeted)
- Search: `site:scholar.google.com "professor" "machine learning" "Stanford"`
- Collect: Name, University, h-index, Research interests, Email (if public)
- **Manual — do 20/day across domains**

### Source 4: University Faculty Pages
Target 10 universities × 10 professors each = 100 profiles
```
MIT, Stanford, Harvard, Oxford, Cambridge, ETH Zurich,
NUS Singapore, IIT Bombay, University of Toronto, TU Berlin
```
Each university has a public faculty directory. Collect name, department, research area, profile URL.

## Phase 3: Onboarding Questions (Day 4–5)

These drive your recommendation engine. Use this pre-built set:

### Section A: Identity (4 questions)
1. What is your current role? *(Student / PhD Researcher / Professor / Industry Researcher / Independent)*
2. What is your highest degree? *(Bachelor's / Master's / PhD / Postdoc)*
3. Which country/institution are you affiliated with?
4. How many years of research experience do you have? *(0–1 / 2–5 / 5–10 / 10+)*

### Section B: Research Focus (5 questions)
5. What is your primary research domain? *(multi-select from 30 categories)*
6. What are your specific research keywords/topics? *(free text, up to 5 tags)*
7. What stage are your current projects at? *(Ideation / Data Collection / Analysis / Writing / Published)*
8. What type of research do you primarily do? *(Empirical / Theoretical / Computational / Review / Mixed)*
9. Are you currently working on a thesis/dissertation? *(Yes – Undergrad / Yes – PhD / No)*

### Section C: Collaboration Goals (5 questions)
10. What are you looking for? *(Co-author / Supervisor / Mentee / Peer reviewer / Study group / Funding partner)*
11. What is your preferred collaboration style? *(Remote only / Hybrid / In-person preferred)*
12. Are you open to international collaboration? *(Yes / Preferred region only / No)*
13. How much time can you commit per week? *(<5 hrs / 5–10 hrs / 10–20 hrs / Full-time)*
14. Do you need help with specific skills? *(Statistics / Writing / Coding / Lab work / Literature review)*

### Section D: Publication & Journals (4 questions)
15. Have you published before? *(Yes – Q1/Q2 / Yes – Q3/Q4 / Conference only / No)*
16. Which journal quartiles are you targeting? *(Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4 / Any)*
17. What citation style does your field use? *(APA / IEEE / Vancouver / Chicago / Other)*
18. Are you interested in open-access publishing? *(Yes / No / Doesn't matter)*

### Section E: Community Participation (3 questions)
19. What kind of content would you post? *(Questions / Answers / Paper summaries / Job posts / Events)*
20. What format of help do you prefer giving/receiving? *(Text answers / Video calls / Async feedback / Code review)*
21. Would you like to be listed as publicly discoverable? *(Full profile / Limited / No)*

## Key Advice

**Don't build profiles manually.** Use OpenAlex and ORCID APIs — you can get 100 quality researcher profiles in an afternoon with a simple script. The journal data is even easier since it's bulk CSV download.

For your beta, **quality over quantity matters more** — 100 well-tagged researchers across 8 domains is far more useful than 500 incomplete profiles. Focus on completeness of each record: name, institution, domain, keywords, and a profile link.
